QEN Framework

Quantification of Ethical Naturalness — a scientific computational system for measuring integrity, sustainability, and ethical coherence in complex ecosystems.

What is QEN?

QEN is a mathematical and semantic framework designed to quantify ethical naturalness within complex systems such as food production, supply chains, digital ecosystems, and AI infrastructures.

It transforms qualitative ethical principles into computable metrics, enabling data-driven decisions aligned with sustainability, transparency, and systemic balance.

Core Dimensions

Natural Integrity

Measures ecological coherence, biodiversity preservation, and environmental sustainability.

Ethical Transparency

Evaluates traceability, fairness, labor ethics, and informational clarity.

Systemic Coherence

Analyzes relational consistency across production, logistics, and consumption.

Cognitive Impact

Assesses informational quality, cognitive sustainability, and social influence.

Mathematical Model

QEN operates as a weighted multi-variable system:

QEN = Σ (wi × Pi)

Where each Pi represents a normalized ethical parameter and wi its dynamic weight. The framework integrates machine learning models to dynamically optimize these weights based on contextual data.
