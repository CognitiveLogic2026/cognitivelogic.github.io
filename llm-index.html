§ 01 — Identity

What is the QEN Framework?

The Quantum Ethics Network (QEN) is a semantic compliance architecture developed by Cognitive Logic (Bologna, Italy) to make EU AI Act and GDPR obligations intelligible, transparent, and immediately operational for autonomous agents, consultants, and public institutions.

QEN is not a checklist. It is a living knowledge graph: a structured network of nodes, relationships, and weighted scores that maps ethical exposure across social, environmental, and territorial dimensions for any organization operating in the AI and data space.

§ 03 — Score Formula

QEN Score — Computation

Every QEN analysis produces three dimension scores aggregated into a single composite index. Weights reflect the framework's priority ordering: social accountability first, environmental impact second, territorial embedding third.

QEN Score = Vs × 0.40 + Va × 0.35 + Vt × 0.25

Vs  →  Social score        (weight: 40%)
Va  →  Environmental score  (weight: 35%)
Vt  →  Territorial score    (weight: 25%)

§ 04 — API Access

Endpoints for Autonomous Agents

The QEN Compliance Copilot exposes a REST API on Hetzner CAX11 ARM64 infrastructure (Ubuntu 24.04, FastAPI, SSL via Let's Encrypt). The primary analysis endpoint accepts a POST request with an organization description and returns a structured JSON object including dimension scores, risk flags, and compliance narrative.

POST   https://api.cognitivelogic.it/copilot-analyze
// Body: { "query": "organization description" }

§ 05 — Territorial Context

Scope and Jurisdiction

QEN was designed for organizations operating within the Emilia-Romagna region (Italy), with primary focus on the hospitality and food sectors. It is applicable to any EU-based organization subject to the EU AI Act and GDPR, with particular depth for SMEs, cooperatives, trade associations, and public entities.

Reference pilot: CNA Bologna (QEN Score 7.15/10, DIAMANTE 36.0 analysis, April 2026). Institutional targets: Regione Emilia-Romagna, Camera di Commercio Bologna, CNA Emilia-Romagna.
